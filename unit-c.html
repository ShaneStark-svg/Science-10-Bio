The Microscope & Cell Theory

Cell theory was built on microscopy. Before microscopes, people believed life could appear from nothing (spontaneous generation). Scientists like Redi (1668) and Pasteur (1859) proved this wrong.

Cell Theory

Louis Pasteur's swan-neck flask experiment (1859) proved that microorganisms come from the air — not spontaneous generation. This confirmed the third principle of cell theory.

Diffusion & Osmosis

Cells constantly exchange materials with their environment through the cell membrane. No energy is needed — particles move naturally from where there are more of them to where there are fewer.

Photosynthesis

Plants, algae, and cyanobacteria convert light energy into chemical energy stored as glucose. This is the foundation of almost all food chains on Earth.

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Carbon dioxide + Water + Light → Glucose + Oxygen

Cellular Respiration

Every living cell breaks down glucose to release usable energy as ATP. This is essentially the reverse of photosynthesis, and happens 24/7 in every living cell.

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP

Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water + Energy (ATP)

Cell Division: Stages of Mitosis

One cell becomes two genetically identical daughter cells. Includes interphase — the essential preparation phase that comes before mitosis itself.
