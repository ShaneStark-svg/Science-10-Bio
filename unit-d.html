Earth's Energy Budget

Of every 100 units of solar energy arriving at Earth, only part of it actually heats the surface. The rest is reflected or absorbed by the atmosphere.

The Greenhouse Effect

A natural and essential process — but human activities are intensifying it, trapping more heat than Earth's systems are used to.

Weather vs. Climate

"Climate is what you expect; weather is what you get."

Weather vs. Climate — Key Differences
FeatureWeatherClimate
DefinitionShort-term atmospheric conditions at a specific place and timeLong-term average of weather patterns over 30+ years
TimeframeHours to daysDecades to centuries
ExamplesToday's snowstorm, Tuesday's chinook windAlberta's cold winters; Amazon's year-round heat
Measured byTemperature, wind speed, humidity, precipitation todayAverages, extremes, and trends over decades
PredictabilityForecasts reliable ~7 days aheadTrends modelled over years and decades
Who studies it?Meteorologists (weather forecasters)Climatologists (climate scientists)
Alberta exampleA chinook raises Calgary's temp by 20°C in 3 hoursAlberta's average winter temp has risen ~3°C since 1950

Major Biomes of the World

A biome is a large geographic region defined by its climate and the characteristic plants and animals adapted to it. Climate determines biome; biome determines what can live there.

Alberta's Climate Zones

Alberta sits within four major natural regions — a great local case study for how climate shapes ecosystems.
